Behavior

The inner workings and social behavior of the emperor penguin are another large contributing factor to the success the penguin has seen in our cold south Antarctic continent. Emperor penguins lay one egg a year. The male penguin will sit on the egg, having it rest on top of his feet and fold his body over it to keep it warm for an incubation period of over two months. The penguin will lose up to half of his body weight as he does not eat during this time. The males will all huddle together within walls of ice or cliffs. This allows them to try and block some of the wind which can be deadly. Their combined body heat and alternating positions keeps the colony from freezing. If the egg hatches before the mother returns from her long hunt the father will feed the chick with his crop milk.

When the mother returns the father, penguin is usually hesitant to leave but will eventually go hunt for food for himself and his chick.
