Eat or be Eaten

The emperor penguin eats fish, crustaceans and cephalopods. Fish are the main source of food as the Antarctic silverfish (Pleuragramma antarcticum) makes up the majority of the birds diet. They are known to feed on squid and krill. They dive 50 m into the frozen ocean depths to search for prey, and they can do this about a dozen times before surfacing for air. The emperor penguins show great prowess under water and use their speed and remarkable agility to evade predators like sea lions and orca’s. They are much more graceful underwater then on land as they are slow to move and hobble around or use their bellies as sleighs. The emperor penguin’s greatest predator however is the Southern Giant Petrels (Macronectes giganteus). This bird is known to be responsible for 1/3erd of the chick deaths in a colony.
