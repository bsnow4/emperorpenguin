Evolved to survive

The emperor penguin has adapted to its environment in order to survive. It has done this even at an evolutionary level. Having a greater density of feathers and down feathers helps keep the emperor penguin from freezing. The emperor penguin is known to be able to withstand -40 degrees Celsius. They also have a layer of fat which can be up to 3cm. They have muscles that are able to lift their feathers while on land and trap air between the bird and the outside, they can then use this to keep themselves warm. These same feathers can be flattened to create a waterproof layer which is great while in the water.

The emperor penguin is able to shut down vital organs that are not being used. Its bones are not filled with air and allow the birds to dive to depths unprecedented for most creatures (recorded at 265-535m below surface level). They are able to do this by lowering their heart beat to 15-20 beats per minute. Having a streamlined body with wings that act as flippers the emperor penguin can swim at speeds of 6-9km an hour. Allowing them to dive to great depths to reach the food they need. Male emperor penguins also have an internal gland in their oesophagus which produces what is known as crop milk. They use this crop milk to feed newly hatched eggs until the mother returns with food from the ocean.
