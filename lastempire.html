The last Empire

The emperor penguin faces one of, if not the harshest environment the world has and its evolutionary biology play a large role in that survival. The emperor penguin has adapted and learned to survive when many other animals would not. They are thought to have come from New Zealand but this could be a misconception as the King Penguin is VERY similar in appearance and habitats New Zealand and other country’s that spawn the southernmost peninsulas of our planet. It’s thought that all penguins have branched off from the emperor penguin over 40 million years ago.

The one thing that the emperor penguin cannot survive is climate change and the impacts from over fishing has done to their food supplies. We cannot expect 40 million years of evolutionary to be able to undo itself to survive in a changing world. This has recently changed our conservation status to near threatened. Some scientists believe the emperor penguin may be extinct by 2100.
